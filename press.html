The story

Korean skincare built the most sophisticated skin-reading culture in the world — 12-tone personal color consultations, separate water and oil axes, five different words for "glow." Almost none of it survives translation to American apps, which still ask five quiz questions and hand you one of four labels.

Jineo is one person's attempt to translate the real framework, honestly. It was designed and built solo — no team, no funding — around one rule borrowed from what makes Korea's beauty review culture trusted: the score can't be bought. No brand pays for placement, rankings, or scores, anywhere in the app.

It's also built for the skin American beauty tech overlooks: marks and hyperpigmentation on melanin-rich skin are treated as first-class signals, not edge cases.

Story angles

  • The anti-sponsored-score app — a skincare app whose whole pitch is that nobody can pay it for a better number.

  • Skin of color as the main character — why hyperpigmentation-first design is still rare in AI skincare.
  • Privacy-first biometrics — a face-scan app that deletes the face.
